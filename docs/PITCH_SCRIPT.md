## PRODUCTION NOTES

### Recording
- **Screen recording:** Use OBS or Loom. Record at 1080p minimum. 60fps preferred for smooth typing animation.
- **Audio:** Record narration separately with a decent microphone. No laptop built-in mic. No background noise. No echo.
- **Face:** No talking head needed. This is a product video, not a vlog. If you want personality, use your voice — not your face.

### Editing
- **Pacing:** Each section must flow naturally. No dead air. No rushed transitions.
- **Text on screen:** Large, readable, high contrast. White text on dark backgrounds or dark text on white. No gray on gray.
- **Confidence bars animation:** When confidence changes (52% → 94%), animate the bar. It's the most satisfying moment in the whole video. Make it feel impactful.
- **Crisis overlay:** This section should feel different. Slower. More serious. The silence after "Some things are too important for AI to decide" is intentional. Let it breathe.

### What NOT to do
- Don't start with "Hi, we're Amine and Harshit." Judges don't care about you yet. They care about the problem.
- Don't explain what AI is. Judges are AI experts. They want to see YOUR AI, not a tutorial.
- Don't use buzzwords without substance. "We leverage machine learning" = nothing. "We classify needs across multiple categories with calibrated confidence scores" = something.
- Don't show bugs. If something glitches during recording, re-record that section.
- Don't go over 3 minutes. Judges have hundreds of videos. Respect their time.
