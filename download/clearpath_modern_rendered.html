THE PROBLEM

A confident wrong answer is more dangerous than no answer at all.

Someone types 'I can't pay rent.' A search engine gives rental assistance. An AI recommends a 3-month waitlist. But what they needed was legal aid for an emergency eviction, available within 24 hours.

The confident match just cost them their home.

03

THE INSIGHT

Most AI systems hide their uncertainty. In community resource matching, that is dangerous.

What if the AI showed you what it knows, what it doesn't, and why?

An honest uncertain answer can save a life. A confident wrong answer can destroy one. Uncertainty is not a bug. It is the safety feature.

04

THE SOLUTION

ClearPath AI

A community resource navigator that shows calibrated confidence instead of hiding uncertainty.

Six layers of intelligent transparency:

1. Free-text input - Describe your situation in your own words

2. Crisis safety net - Hardcoded detection, AI cannot override

3. Multi-label classification - Multiple matches with confidence scores

4. Clarification questions - When confidence is low, ask, don't guess

5. Transparent reasoning - Every recommendation shows why and how confident

6. Human escalation - When AI is unsure, connect to a real person

07

RESPONSIBLE AI

Responsibility is the architecture, not a disclaimer.

Three built-in safety mechanisms:

1. Crisis safety net - Hardcoded keyword detection that the AI layer cannot override. Crisis resources always appear.

2. Forced transparency - Confidence scores are always visible. The system never presents a single answer as certain when it isn't.

3. Automatic human escalation - When no category exceeds 70% confidence, the system recommends a trained navigator instead of guessing harder.

Known limitations

Cannot determine eligibility. Cannot replace social workers. May misclassify complex multi-need situations. Requires curated local data.
